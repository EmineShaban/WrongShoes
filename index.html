How Poor Foot Hygiene & Wrong Shoes Resulted In Toenail Fungus

Hello, my name is Emma. I’m here to share my cautionary tale about dealing with toenail fungus. It’s never an easy subject to broach but I decided to help others avoid making my mistakes and suffering for it.

My problems started with seemingly innocent mistakes in foot hygiene and poor shoe choices. It might sound trivial and silly, but by the end of my story, you’ll understand the importance of maintaining clean, dry feet and wearing comfortable, practical shoes.

It all started with what I thought were minor inconveniences - a busy lifestyle, neglecting my feet’s hygiene and, occasionally, opting for fashionable yet impractical shoes that caused my feet to get hot and sweaty.

Little did I know that these seemingly harmless choices would lead to a struggle with toenail fungus.

As my days were turning busier, I paid less attention to the well-being of my feet. I would often neglect to -

  • dry them thoroughly after a shower,
  • avoid walking barefoot in my gym’s locker room, or
  • abstain from wearing closed shoes for extended periods without allowing my feet to breathe.

I didn’t realize I was creating the ideal breeding ground for the stubborn, pesky fungi.

The first signs of trouble appeared subtly -

  • a slight discoloration
  • a bit of itching
  • a strange smell, and
  • a tiny discomfort.

I brushed it all off, thinking it was just a passing issue and nothing to worry about.

However, as weeks went by, my toenail fungus escalated.

The once minor discomfort turned into persistent pain, the discoloration spread making my toenails awful to look at, and my toenails had become extremely thick.

I started noticing my poor shoe choices played a significant role in aggravating the situation. Those stylish but uncomfortable shoes that I loved so much were trapping moisture, providing the perfect environment for the fungus to thrive. It was a painful realization that fashion was taking a toll on my foot health.

Walking, which used to be a simple and pain-free activity, was now a daily struggle. Wearing open-toed shoes was out of the question, leading to my confidence dropping rapidly. I decided it was time to address the issue before it became worse.

After realizing my mistakes, I settled on a two-way approach to deal with my toenail fungus problem -

  • Various alternative treatments. Over-the-counter creams and home remedies involving vinegar and baking soda, which ended up being quite painful, were slightly helpful but did not treat the root cause of the fungal infection.
  • Podiatrist visit. To my absolute horror, the specialist recommended invasive treatments such as medical pedicures and laser treatment. I was terrified and yet, I didn’t waste time and made the costly appointments. After the painful procedures, the relief was temporary and short-lived. My toenail fungus was not cured.
